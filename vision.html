Vision

My vision of the future

Where I think marketing technology — and my own work — is heading, and the kind of impact I want to have getting there.

The short version

One eye on delivery, one on what's next

I'm a Salesforce and martech person by trade, but what really drives me is what's coming next. The tools are changing fast — AI, unified customer data, autonomous agents — and I want to be at the front of that shift, not catching up to it.

My bet is simple: the brands that win will be the ones that pair genuinely powerful technology with a real, human understanding of the people on both sides of the screen — the customers being engaged, and the teams doing the work.

That belief is why I keep stacking certifications, take on migrations most people avoid, and build side projects on the weekend. It's all the same habit — learning by doing, and keeping an eye on the horizon.

What I'm betting on

Three things I think the next few years are about

AI-native marketing

Marketing Cloud, Data Cloud and Einstein / Agentforce are converging into something new — journeys that are planned, personalised and optimised with AI by default. I want to help brands adopt this responsibly: using AI to remove the busywork, not the human judgement that makes marketing actually land.

Data that actually connects

Unified, well-governed customer data is the foundation everything else stands on. My focus is helping teams turn scattered, messy data into a clear, actionable and privacy-respecting understanding of their customers — the difference between guessing and knowing.

People-first delivery

Technology should give teams back time and energy for creativity and care. With a psychology background and agile habits, I want to keep delivery humane — clear, calm and collaborative — even as the tooling underneath gets more powerful and more automated.

Where I'm headed

From hands-on delivery to shaping how it's done

Personally, I want to keep growing from hands-on delivery toward shaping how organisations adopt martech and AI — as a consultant and leader who can bridge the technical, the commercial and the human sides of a project.

My certifications, the migrations I take on at VML MAP, and side projects like Keto Scanner are all part of that same direction of travel: stay curious, build things, and help the teams and brands I work with feel confident about what's coming next instead of anxious about it.

If you're looking for someone who keeps one eye on delivery today and one on what's next, I'd love to talk.
